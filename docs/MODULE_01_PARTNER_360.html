Module Overview

partner_360.py

Partner 360 is a two-step drill-down interface: the user first selects a State/Region, then selects a specific partner from that state. The system then calls ai.get_partner_intelligence(partner_name) and renders a multi-section report.

The report covers:

  • Revenue health (90d vs prior 90d, drop %, monthly loss estimate)
  • Churn Risk Intelligence v2 (composite score, top signal, peer benchmark, outreach script)
  • Credit risk score, band, utilization, overdue ratio, outstanding amount
  • Cross-sell peer gap analysis (ranked by monthly gap value)
  • SPIN selling framework auto-generated from live data
  • Top 5 similar partners by SVD cosine similarity
  • PDF and Excel export
🎯
Design Principle The goal is to compress a full pre-call research session (20–30 min) into a 30-second tab load. Every section provides a recommended action, not just a metric.

Churn Risk Intelligence v2

The churn intelligence block renders only for partners in the At Risk or Critical segment. It uses a composite score (0–100) built from multiple behavioral signals, each with a severity level (critical / high / medium).

Composite Score Components

SignalSeverity TriggerAction
Revenue Stoppedrecent_90 = 0, prev_90 > 0Immediate escalation call
High Recency (≥120d)CriticalOutreach with incentive offer
Revenue Drop ≥50%CriticalInvestigate cause, offer credit review
Category AbandonmentHighRe-introduce lost category
Order Volume DropHigh / MediumProbe for supply friction
AOV DeclineMediumUpsell or bundle to lift basket size

Score Gauge Bar

The composite score (0–100) is rendered as a colored progress bar. Color thresholds: Critical ≥80, High ≥55, Medium ≥30, Low <30.

Peer Benchmark Row

Shows the partner's 90d revenue vs cluster average, with category breadth delta and peer average recency. Source: report["churn_reasons"]["peer_context"].

Recovery Potential & Outreach Script

A recovery label (formatted INR) shows estimated yearly recovery if the partner returns to their historical average. An outreach script is generated by the clustering engine and displayed as a collapsed code block for easy copy-paste.

⚠️
Dual Schema Support churn_reasons can be either a list of signals (old format) or a dict with composite_score/signals/peer_context (v2 format). The UI handles both via duck-typing checks.

Peer Gap Analysis (Cross-Sell)

The gap table compares this partner's product-group purchasing against the cluster average. Products where the partner spends significantly less than cluster peers represent cross-sell opportunities.

Gap_Monthly = (Peer_Avg_Spend − Partner_Spend_in_Group) × partner_scaling_factor Gap_Yearly = Gap_Monthly × 12 # Tiered display: 🔥 High Priority : Gap_Monthly ≥ ₹50,000 ⚡ Medium Priority : Gap_Monthly ∈ [₹10,000, ₹50,000) Low Priority : Gap_Monthly < ₹10,000 (collapsed expander)
ColumnDescription
ProductProduct group name (group_name from master_group)
Gap (Monthly)Estimated monthly revenue gap vs peer average
Gap (Yearly)Annualized gap
You Do% of peers who buy this product group
Peers Do% of cluster peers who buy this product group

💡
Perfect Match If the partner's buying matches peer averages exactly, the gaps table will be empty and the UI shows "Perfect account. Matches peer average." Outlier/uncategorized partners show a different warning.

SPIN Selling Script Engine

The SPIN framework (Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff) is auto-generated from live partner data. Each block is context-aware:

S
Situation

Opens with what we know — recency, cluster type, cluster name, region context. Builds rapport from facts, not assumptions.

P
Problem

If revenue drop > 5%: surfaces the decline without confrontation. If cross-sell gap: highlights what peers are buying that this partner isn't.

I
Implication

Makes the cost of inaction tangible — either the yearly revenue gap in INR, or elevated churn risk phrased as losing supplier priority.

N
Need-Payoff

Closes on a concrete, low-friction action (trial allocation, no minimum commitment) — not a vague offer. Optionally mentions credit term adjustment.

Similar Partners (SVD Similarity)

If ai.get_similar_partners() is available (requires graph_embeddings_mixin), the top 5 most similar partners are displayed, ranked by cosine similarity in SVD-reduced product-group embedding space.

Similarity = cosine(SVD_vector[partner_A], SVD_vector[partner_B]) # Matrix = partner × product_group spend (after TF-IDF-like normalisation) # SVD reduces to ~50 dimensions # Useful for identifying substitute accounts or replication targets
🔬
Why SVD, not Cluster Label? Cluster labels are coarse (4–8 groups). SVD similarity captures fine-grained buying pattern overlap — two "At Risk" partners can have very different product mix, while two partners in different segments can be near-identical buyers.
